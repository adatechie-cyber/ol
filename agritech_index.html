Strategic Dialogue on the Future of EU Agriculture

100% Remote · Asynchronous Video & Interactive Digital Posters

Context & Objective

Designed for PhD students, researchers, and practitioners from conflict-affected and low-income settings where travel is restricted. Addressing war-driven food crises across global supply chains via low-tech biotech and climate-adaptive systems.

Track A: Crop, Forestry & Resource Systems

  • Adaptation strategies within crop and forestry production matrices.
  • Optimization models for resource use efficiency to drive localized productivity.
  • Evidence-based impact assessment pipelines mapping biotechnology for smallholders.

Track B: Deep-Tech, Policy & Post-Production

  • Livestock and aquaculture adaptation parameters under volatile climate states.
  • Post-production value enhancement architectures and rigorous food safety protocols.
  • Tech-to-Market integration systems and strategic framework dynamics for AI-driven sustainability.
